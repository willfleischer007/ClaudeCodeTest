Key Scoring Insights

SV/HD Premium Is the Biggest Edge

A 1-inning save appearance nets: 3.0 (IP) + 1.6 (K) + 7.2 (SV) = ~11.8 pts — more than an average starter's 3 full innings. Elite closers are worth roughly $35–45 in this format, and high-leverage setup men are worth $15–25. HD at 4.5 pts is worth targeting in non-save roles aggressively.

Contact Hitter vs. Power Hitter Value

The AB penalty and K penalty combine to heavily tax strikeout-heavy profiles. A hitter with 200 Ks generates 200 × −0.7 = −140 pts in K/AB losses. A contact hitter with 80 Ks saves 120 points annually before counting the actual hits. High-contact, high-walk players (similar to Nico Hoerner) are systematically undervalued in this format vs. traditional roto.

SB Is Pure Positive EV

At +2.0 per SB with no AB cost, and CS only −0.5, a runner with 75% success rate needs only 1 SB per 3 attempts to break even. Any runner above 70% success with 20+ opportunities is a net positive. Speed skills are underpriced in this format — prioritize players with elite sprint speed.

QS Value Has Declined — IP Now Dominates

As league-average SP IP/start has dropped toward 5.2 innings, QS rates are falling. The real value from starters is 3.0 pts per IP compounding — a 200-IP arm generates 600 pts from IP alone before Ks. Target durable arms over boom-bust high-K pitchers with injury histories.

GIDP Is the Hidden Killer

At −1.2 net per GIDP, slow left-handed pull hitters in front of middling lineups lose 15–25 pts annually from this stat alone. Check Statcast ground ball rates and GIDP frequency for any player you're considering at a discount.

C. Inflation Calculation

In a keeper league, players retained below market value reduce the supply of talent available at auction while the total spending pool only decreases by keeper salaries. This creates inflation — available auction dollars chase fewer high-quality players.

Step-by-Step Inflation Model

1
Total League Budget: 10 teams × $260 = $2,600
2
Your Keeper Salaries: Sum of all your kept players = $213 (see keeper table below)
3
Estimated League-Wide Keeper Salaries: Assuming 9 other teams average ~$110 in keeper commitments = $990 + $213 = ~$1,203 total
⚠ Update this with actual league keeper data for precise calculation
4
Auction Pool Remaining: $2,600 − $1,203 = $1,397 available to spend at auction
5
Real Value of Keepers at Market: Estimating keepers league-wide represent ~$1,650 in true market value (kept at below-market deals) = Value remaining in player pool: $2,600 − $1,650 = $950 in real player value
6
Inflation Multiplier: $1,397 ÷ $950 = 1.47× — approximately 47% inflation
7
Applying Inflation: Multiply any player's standard $260 auction value by 1.47 to get their expected auction clearing price in your league.
Example Inflation Adjustments:
José Ramírez (base $38) → ~$56 auction value
Cal Raleigh (base $32) → ~$47 auction value
Spencer Strider (base $22) → ~$32 auction value
Mid-tier SP (base $12) → ~$18 auction value
Streaming starter (base $3) → ~$4–5 auction value

Holds Value Analysis — The Underrated Asset

At 4.5 pts per HD, a reliever collecting 30 holds per season generates 135 hold points. Add 60 IP × 3.0 = 180 pts, plus 70 K × 1.6 = 112 pts = roughly 427 total points from a high-leverage setup man. Compare this to a No. 4 starter who might generate 350–400 pts on the season. High-leverage non-closer relievers are chronically undervalued in this format. Budget $8–15 for one elite setup arm as a second bullpen piece.

Stars & Scrubs vs. Balanced Approach

In this scoring format, Stars & Scrubs favors your situation. You already have a deep keeper base providing consistent floor. Your auction dollars should be concentrated on one or two premium targets (3B and a closer) rather than spread thin across 4–5 mid-tier players. Points leagues with high scoring rates reward roster dominance at key positions more than balanced mediocrity.

In-Auction Tactical Notes

Nominate Your Non-Targets First

Early in the auction, nominate elite catchers (Cal Raleigh), elite OF, and players you don't need. Let other teams deplete their budgets chasing positions you're already covered at. Then strike in the middle portion of the auction when budgets are reduced but your specific targets (3B, closer) haven't been exhausted.

Inflation Creates Price Anchoring Traps

With ~47% inflation, players will seem expensive vs. their base values. Don't anchor to pre-inflation prices — $40+ for a Tier 1 closer is correct in your market. The mistake is paying $35 for a Tier 2 arm thinking you're getting a deal when you're actually overpaying at 1.47×.

The Late Auction Endgame

Reserve $8–12 for the final 20% of the auction. Late auction presents the best value — teams run out of budget and elite players go for $1–3 due to budget constraints. Your keeper savings give you budget flexibility to exploit this window. Have a list of 5–8 "price is right" targets for the final bidding phase.
